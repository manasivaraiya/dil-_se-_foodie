Chana Bhatura

Chana Bhatura is a spicy, tasty and a filling dish. I have eaten this dish at many places in Punjab and Delhi. Each restaurants or dhaba (eateries on highways) have their own flavors and taste in the chickpea curry. In some places the curry is very spicy, at other places ithas tangy taste and the consistency of the curry also varies from slightly thick to semi-dry and dry...

  • 2 to 3 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • 1⁄2 teaspoon turmeric powder,1⁄4 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder, 1⁄2 or 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur powder) 1 to 1.5 cups water to be added later, salt as required
  • 1 green chilli (hari mirch) - slit, 1 teaspoon garam masala powder or chole masala, whole garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 large tej patta (indian bay leaf) 2 inch cinnamon, 2 to 3 cloves, 2 green cardamom

  • 1⁄3 cup fine sooji (rava or fine semolina) 1⁄2 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • 1⁄2 tablespoon fine granulated sugar
  • 1⁄2 teaspoon salt or as required, 1⁄4 or 1⁄2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1⁄2 cup curd (yogurt)
  • water - as required for kneading, oil - for deep frying as required

Dum Paneer

Dum Paneer is an authentic dish which is perfect to serve on all occasions. This recipe of Dum Paneer will not require much time to prepare in your own kitchen or exclusive ingredients to cook but it is still very delicious and has an amazing aroma.This is a must-have vegetarian dish on your next dinner/lunch party which will leave your guests asking for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 Cloves
  • 4 Cardamom
  • 1 Cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp ginger
  • 1 tsp garlic
  • 1/2 tsp green Chillies
  • 3 tbsp curd
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin ppowder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 20 Paneer cubes
  • 2 tbsp cream
  • 1/4 tsp black peppercorn (crushed)
  • Water (as per need)

Procedure


1.Heat oil and add the cloves, cardamoms
and cinnamon till it's nice and fragrant.
2.Now add onion paste and cook well.
3.Add ginger, garlic and green chilli paste.Saute.
4.Add yogurt / dahi.
5.Cook and add coriander powder, cumin powder,
 pepper powder, haldi and rang mirch / red chilli.
6.Add salt. Cook and add cottage cheese cubes and cream 
as well as 1/2 cup water.
7.Seal the pan with foil and put a lid on top.
8.Reduce flame to low and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
9.Now open it and cook further if you like to dry it up.
10.Check for salt adding more if needed, serve garnished 
with coriander and mint leaves.

Dal Tadka

Dal Tadka is a popular Indian lentil dish made with arhar dal or masoor dal and tempered in oil with spices and herbs. In this post I am sharing 2 versions of making dal tadka.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup chane ki dal (Bengal gram)
  • 5 cups water
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tsp haldi (turmeric)
  • 2 tsp ginger, chopped
  • 1 tsp sugar or to taste
  • 1 tbsp nariyal pieces (heaped), pan fried
  • 2 tbsp sarson ka tel
  • 1 tsp sarson (mustard seeds)
  • 2-3 Sabut lal mirch
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Procedure

1.Pick, clean and wash the dal and cook
with the water, salt, haldi and ginger,
 till soft.
2.Add the sugar and fried nariyal.
3.Heat oil and add the sarson and lal mirch.
4.Add the dal and bring to a boil, and then simmer 
for a minute.
5.Serve hot garnished with the coriander leaves.
